Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyberattacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

The first major cybersecurity event occured on November 2, 1988

Importance of Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is crucial because it helps protect sensitive data, maintain the integrity of systems, and ensure the availability of services. Without proper cybersecurity measures, organizations and individuals are vulnerable to data breaches, financial loss, and reputational damage.

Common Cybersecurity Threats

Some common cybersecurity threats include malware, phishing attacks, ransomware, and DoS attacks. These threats can compromise the security of systems and data, leading to significant consequences for individuals and organizations.

Some more advanced threats include zero-day exploits, advanced persistent threats (APTs), and insider threats.

Security professionals often use Wireshark to analyze network traffic.

Security controls should be implemented based on the organization's risk assessment and tailored to meet the specific security requirements of the information system and its environment of operation.

— NIST Special Publication 800-53
Protecting Against Cybersecurity Threats

To protect against cybersecurity threats, it is important to implement strong security measures such as firewalls, antivirus software, regular software updates, and employee training on security best practices. Additionally, using strong passwords and enabling MFA can help safeguard sensitive information.
